OUR HISTORY!

Find out about our wonderful history

In the heart of Europe, the history of France unfolds as a captivating story of eras rich in events and transformations. From the times of the ancient Gauls to the glory of the Romans, passing through the Middle Ages marked by kings and knights, France has left an indelible mark on historical narrative. The French Revolution, with its motto liberté, égalité, fraternité, resonated as an echo of change and emancipation. Over the centuries, this nation has forged its cultural, artistic and political identity, becoming a beacon of influence that illuminates the global panorama.

Roman age

In ancient times, France was known as Gaul and was conquered by Julius Caesar in 50 B.C. The region was integrated into the Roman Empire, experiencing cultural and social influences that left a lasting imprint.

Middle Ages

During the Middle Ages, Gaul witnessed barbarian invasions and the formation of the Carolingian Kingdom under the rule of Charlemagne in the 9th century. The Treaty of Verdun in 843 divided the Carolingian empire, marking the emergence of France as an entity independent.

Hundred Years' War (1337-1453)

The Hundred Years' War was a prolonged conflict between France and England that left a deep mark on history. Figures such as Joan of Arc played a role crucial in the French resistance, consolidating national identity.

Renaissance and Reformation

The 15th and 16th centuries witnessed a cultural and artistic flourishing in France. However, there were also religious conflicts, such as the Wars of Religion, which They marked the struggle between Catholics and Protestants.
